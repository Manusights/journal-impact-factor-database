# Journal Selection System Guide

This guide gives a practical system for choosing journals with less guesswork and fewer failed submission rounds.

## Why most journal selection fails
A lot of teams choose journals by prestige first, then try to force-fit the paper. That usually creates desk rejects because scope, claim strength, and evidence depth are not aligned.

A stronger approach is to choose journals with a structured scorecard.

## Build a scorecard
Use four dimensions:

1. Scope fit (0 to 35)
2. Claim and evidence fit (0 to 25)
3. Operational fit, including review timeline (0 to 20)
4. Audience fit and visibility goals (0 to 20)

Total score is 100.

## Step-by-step workflow

### Step 1: Define non-negotiables
- Study type and topic scope
- Funder or institution open-access requirements
- Maximum acceptable review cycle

### Step 2: Build longlist
Collect 20 to 40 journals from your field. Exclude obvious mismatches fast.

### Step 3: Score each journal
Score with short evidence notes. If two scorers disagree, discuss score differences before final ranking.

### Step 4: Create submission ladder
- Target 1: best strategic fit
- Target 2: realistic fit
- Target 3: fast fallback fit

### Step 5: Pre-submit quality gate
Before submission, confirm:
- claim-to-figure mapping is complete
- reference integrity check passed
- methods detail supports core claims

## Common failure patterns
- Scope fit is weak but impact factor is high
- Main conclusion is stronger than evidence
- Title and abstract oversell novelty
- Submission package lacks a clear audience rationale

## How to use this with your tracker
Use `templates/journal_shortlist_template.csv` and keep one line per candidate journal with score, main risk, and final decision.
